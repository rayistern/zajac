DID YOU KNOW
No iron tools were permitted for the Altar's stones — iron shortens life (war), while the Altar prolongs life (atonement). Even the ramp to the altar had to be built without steps, so the priests wouldn't take wide strides in a disrespectful manner.

The Rebbe's Insights
THE REBBE'S INSIGHT · PEREK 1
Portable vs. Permanent Holiness
The Mishkan was portable — its sanctity came from its components, not the ground it stood on. The Beis HaMikdash is the opposite: the place itself is holy, even without the building.
‏לקוטי שיחות חכ״ט ע׳ 71
THE REBBE'S INSIGHT · PEREK 3
The Ascending Levels of Holiness
The Temple required ascending steps between each level of holiness — the spiritual gradations had to be expressed in physical space. This teaches that spiritual growth must be embodied in concrete, tangible action.
‏לקוטי שיחות חי״א ע׳ 155
