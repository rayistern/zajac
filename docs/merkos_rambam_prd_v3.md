## 1. Executive Summary

This document defines the requirements for a web-based platform that enhances the daily Rambam learning experience through AI-generated visual content, structured sichos references, and a shareable content engine.

The platform targets a narrow, well-defined audience: people who already learn Rambam daily, primarily within Chabad communities following the 1-perek or 3-perek learning tracks. It is not a general Torah education tool. It is a companion for an existing daily practice.

The build is a rapid, low-cost effort by a solo developer leveraging AI tooling. The platform will be validated through a pre-app content distribution phase before any application is built.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Thousands of people learn Rambam daily. The existing digital tools available to them are limited to text display and audio shiurim. No platform currently provides:

- **Visual comprehension aids** for halachos that describe spatial relationships, measurements, processes, or structures (Temple layout, agricultural laws, sacrificial procedures, unit conversions)
- **Structured sichos references** mapped to individual halachos, making the Rebbe's Torah on Rambam accessible in context rather than requiring separate lookup
- **A shareable content engine** that produces compelling visual assets learners and teachers can distribute through their existing channels
- **Interactive learning tools** scoped to the daily material

The result is a daily learning experience that has not meaningfully improved with technology despite advances in AI-generated content, natural language processing, and digital distribution.

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary

| Segment | Description | Need |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **3-perek daily learners** | Engaged individuals learning three chapters of Rambam per day | Deeper understanding of complex halachos, visual aids, sichos connections |
| **1-perek daily learners** | Same commitment, different cadence | Same needs, different track |

### Secondary

| Segment | Description | Need |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Teachers / Maggidei Shiur** | Individuals who prepare and deliver Rambam shiurim | Ready-made visual assets for class preparation and WhatsApp group distribution |

### Passive

| Segment | Description | Need |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Non-learners (social encounter)** | People who encounter shared content on social media or messaging | Self-contained visual content that communicates a Torah concept without requiring the app or prior knowledge |

The teacher segment is likely the highest-leverage distribution channel. Teachers sharing assets to WhatsApp groups puts the content in front of large audiences with implicit endorsement.

The platform is designed for these audiences. Broader expansion (Chidon participants, academic users, non-Chabad communities, multilingual audiences) may be explored in future phases but is not in scope for initial development.

## 4. Solution Overview

### 4.1 Product Description

A Progressive Web App (PWA) that displays the daily Rambam text indexed at the halacha level, accompanied by AI-generated visual content, sichos references, and tools for sharing. Content is generated by AI and reviewed by a human editorial team before publication.

### 4.2 Key Differentiators

| Differentiator | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **AI-generated visual content at daily cadence** | Conceptual images and infographics produced for every day's learning, at a scale no manual process can sustain |
| **Sichos-to-halacha mapping** | The Rebbe's sichos references connected to individual halachos using automated footnote cross-referencing. No existing platform offers this. |
| **Shareable content engine** | Every view on the platform can generate a branded, watermarked, self-contained shareable asset optimized for WhatsApp and social media |

### 4.3 Design Principles

1. **The content is the product.** The application is delivery infrastructure. Content quality determines success or failure.
2. **Narrow and deep over broad and shallow.** Serve daily Rambam learners exceptionally well before expanding to adjacent audiences.
3. **Every shareable asset works at two levels.** For a learner, it aids comprehension. For a non-learner encountering it on social media, it tells a complete, self-contained story.
4. **AI generates, humans validate.** No AI-generated Torah content publishes without human editorial review. No exceptions.

## 5. Phased Delivery

### 5.1 Phase 0 — Content Validation (Pre-App)

**Objective:** Validate that the content has organic pull before building application infrastructure.

**Approach:** Distribute AI-generated visual content through WhatsApp to a small seed audience. Measure engagement, organic forwarding, and unprompted feature requests.

**Deliverables:**
- Automated script generating candidate images for each day's Rambam perakim
- Human curation (selection of best outputs)
- Daily distribution to 3–5 WhatsApp groups and 2–3 teachers

**No application is built in this phase.** No database, hosting, user accounts, or watermarking. The investment is a generation script and a WhatsApp broadcast.

**Duration:** Days to weeks.

**Transition criteria to Phase 1:**

| Signal | Indicator |
|--------|-----------|
| Content has organic pull | Images forwarded beyond seed groups consistently |
| Teacher channel validated | 2+ teachers using assets in shiurim and forwarding to their own groups |
| Demand for product features | Requests for past days, browsable archive, additional context |
| Organic growth | People requesting to join the distribution list without direct invitation |

If none of these signals appear within 30 days, iterate on content quality and format before proceeding.

---

### 5.2 Phase 1 — Core Platform

**Objective:** Deliver a working web application with the core content types and distribution capabilities.

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks for initial build; continuous iteration thereafter.

**Content Scope:**

| Content Type | Description | Generation Method |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Conceptual images** | AI-generated images illustrating complex halachos | Image generation API, style guide, human selection |
| **Infographics** | Structured visual explanations — timelines, flowcharts, comparison tables | Same image generation pipeline, different prompt templates |
| **Sichos highlights** | Rebbe's sichos references mapped to individual halachos | Automated footnote cross-referencing (Rayi's existing method) |
| **Perek overviews** | AI-generated orientation summary for each perek | LLM generation from Rambam text |
| **"Did you know" insights** | Single compelling fact per day, optimized for shareability | LLM generation from Rambam text |
| **Daily charts** | Visual charts generated from each day's content | Rayi's existing chart generation system |

Note: Conceptual images and infographics share the same generation pipeline. They are different output formats, not different features.

**Application Scope:**

| Capability | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Halacha-indexed Rambam text** | Clean text display with individual halachos as the atomic unit |
| **Daily homepage** | Opens to today's content. Navigation to previous and next days. |
| **Track selection** | 1-perek and 3-perek track support with persistent user selection |
| **Shareable assets** | Any content view generates a branded, watermarked card for sharing |
| **WhatsApp delivery** | Daily content package (text + featured image + link to platform) |
| **Watermarking** | Applied automatically to all visual outputs |

**Explicitly out of scope for Phase 1:**

| Item | Rationale |
|------|-----------|
| Chatbot | Highest complexity feature. Grounding and accuracy constraints make it qualitatively different from serving static content. Deferred until the content pipeline is proven and real user questions inform the design. |
| Audio integration | Chabad.org serves this well. No duplication in Phase 1. |
| Multilingual support | Significant effort. Not required for the primary audience. |
| CMS or creator tools | Platform capabilities. Premature before product-market fit. |

## 6. Content Generation Architecture

### 6.1 Pipeline Model

Content is produced through a small number of generation pipelines. Each pipeline is an investment that produces multiple content types. Prioritization follows pipeline ROI — the question is not "which content type ships next" but "which pipeline investment unlocks the most value."

**Visual Pipeline**
Produces: conceptual images, infographics, daily charts.
Shared infrastructure: image generation API, style guide, watermarking, visual review workflow.
Infographics and conceptual images are different prompt templates within the same pipeline, not separate systems.

**Text Pipeline**
Produces: perek overviews, "did you know" insights, quiz questions (Phase 1.5), inline biurim (Phase 2).
Shared infrastructure: LLM API (via PortKey), prompt templates, text review workflow.
Version 1 generates from Rambam text alone. Version 2 (Phase 2) incorporates shiurim content for significantly richer output, particularly for inline biurim.

**Sichos Pipeline**
Produces: sichos highlights mapped to halachos.
Method: automated footnote cross-referencing using Rayi's existing system. Not AI generation — structured data mapping.

**Retrieval Pipeline (Phase 2)**
Produces: internal Rambam cross-references, broader Chassidus connections.
Infrastructure: vector database (ChabadAI shared infrastructure), retrieval-augmented generation.
Extends the text pipeline with a retrieval step upstream.

### 6.2 Shiurim Dependency

Several text pipeline outputs improve substantially when informed by shiurim content rather than generated from Rambam text alone. The shiurim-to-halacha segmentation problem — extracting what each Rav says about each individual halacha — is technically challenging and is not a Phase 1 dependency.

| Content Type | Without Shiurim (Phase 1) | With Shiurim (Phase 2) |
|-------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Perek overview | Serviceable | Richer emphasis on key points |
| "Did you know" | Functional | More compelling insights |
| Quiz questions | Adequate | Better targeted to points of difficulty |
| Inline biurim | Decent | Significantly richer — the primary quality upgrade |

**Decision:** Ship text pipeline v1 without shiurim dependency. Upgrade when the segmentation capability is available. Do not block four content types on the hardest technical problem.

### 6.3 Parallel Activities

**Digitization of reference works:** The Rambam-to-Shulchan Aruch mapping book and Klalei HaRambam methodology book both exist in print and require scanning/OCR for digital integration. This work has no dependency on any pipeline and can proceed at any time.

**Rabbi Zajac's image library:** Existing visual content from years of shiurim. Requires organizing and digitizing. Opportunistic — integrate as available.

## 7. Operations

### 7.1 Daily Content Pipeline

The platform publishes new content every day, 365 days per year, with no exceptions. This is the primary operational commitment and the hardest part of the project.

**Daily workflow:**

| Stage | Process | SLA |
|-------|---------|-----|
| Ingestion | Identify today's perakim for both tracks. Extract halachos. Flag complex halachos for visual treatment. | Automated |
| Generation | Produce candidate images, infographics, text outputs, and sichos mapping for each perek. | < 2 hours |
| Editorial review | Reviewers assess halachic accuracy, hashkafic appropriateness, and visual quality. Rejections return to generation. | 24-hour turnaround |
| Assembly | Assemble web content, WhatsApp package, and shareable assets. Apply watermarks. Automated QA. | < 1 hour |
| Publication | Publish to web. Send WhatsApp delivery. | By earliest global sunset (Australia shkiah) |

**Publication SLA:** Content must be live before the earliest global sunset each day. For Fridays, this means content for Shabbos is reviewed and queued by Thursday. The target content buffer is 7 days ahead of publication.

### 7.2 Editorial Model

All AI-generated content requires human review before publication. The editorial team is volunteer-based.

**Staffing requirements:**
- Minimum 3 reviewers per day
- Accounting for Shabbos, Yom Tov, vacation, illness, and burnout: **6–10 volunteers** needed to sustain a reliable rotation
- Estimated daily time commitment per reviewer: 15–30 minutes

**This is the single largest operational risk.** The volunteer editorial model must be validated with named individuals and confirmed commitments before the platform launches. The following must be defined:

- Named volunteer roster (6–10 individuals)
- Daily rotation schedule
- Expected time commitment, communicated clearly
- Shabbos and Yom Tov coverage protocol
- Fallback procedures when fewer than 3 reviewers are available
- Fatigue prevention (rotation frequency, check-ins)

**Degradation policy:**

| Level | Condition | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Normal | 3+ reviewers approve | Full content package published |
| Reduced | 2 reviewers available | Publish with 2-person review. Internal flag. |
| Minimal | 1 reviewer available | Text and sichos only. No AI-generated visuals. Retroactive review scheduled. |
| Emergency | 0 reviewers available | Rambam text only. No AI content. User-facing notice. |
| Prohibited | — | AI-generated Torah content never publishes with zero human review. |

### 7.3 Content Accuracy

Content accuracy for Torah material is non-negotiable. The acceptable error rate is near zero.

- All AI-generated content is reviewed before publication
- The chatbot (Phase 1.5) is grounded exclusively in approved sources with clear AI disclosure on every response
- A kill switch is available for the chatbot if accuracy issues are detected
- Published errors are corrected immediately; halachic errors receive a visible correction notice
- The 7-day content buffer ensures review is never compromised by time pressure

## 9. Technical Approach

### 9.1 Platform

- **Web-first (PWA).** Ships fastest, works on all devices, avoids app store processes. Natural pairing with WhatsApp link-sharing.
- **Standalone product with shared ChabadAI backend** where applicable (PortKey routing, vector database, chatbot engine when ready).
- Native mobile application may be considered in a future phase if usage patterns justify it.

### 9.2 Build vs. Buy (Phase 1)

| Component | Approach | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Image generation | API (DALL-E / Midjourney / SD) | Fastest path. Evaluate self-hosting if volume justifies. |
| LLM (text generation + chatbot) | API via PortKey | Model flexibility. Grounding and guardrails more important than model choice. |
| WhatsApp delivery | WhatsApp Business API | Standard integration. |
| Editorial workflow | Lightweight tool (Notion / Airtable) | Adequate for Phase 1 volume. Custom CMS only if Phase 2 requires. |
| Analytics | PostHog or Mixpanel | Off-the-shelf. Event tracking from day one. |
| Web application | Custom build (Next.js / PWA) | Required for the specific daily-learning UX, RTL support, and content integration. |
| Rambam text | Sefaria API or equivalent | Open, structured. Halacha-level indexing is the key requirement. |

### 9.3 Cost Structure

Phase 1 build cost is near zero: one developer, AI-assisted development, standard web hosting.

Ongoing costs are dominated by API usage (image generation and LLM) and WhatsApp Business API fees. At Phase 1 volume, these are minimal. Cost optimization (including potential self-hosting of image generation) becomes relevant only if usage scales significantly.

### 9.4 Key Technical Decisions

| Decision | Recommendation | Status |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Standalone product vs. ChabadAI vertical | Standalone with shared backend | Pending confirmation |
| Rambam text source and halacha-level indexing | Evaluate Sefaria API | Pending |
| Chatbot inclusion in Phase 1 vs. 1.5 | Defer to Phase 1.5 | Recommended |

## 10. Dependencies and Risks

### 10.1 Dependencies

| Dependency | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Volunteer editorial team | Cannot launch without confirmed reviewers | Recruit named individuals before launch. Define degradation policy. |
| Rambam text source with halacha-level indexing | Foundation for all content | Evaluate sources early. Halacha indexing is the key technical validation. |
| ChabadAI shared backend (if chosen) | Chatbot and vector DB depend on availability | Confirm availability and SLA. Ensure graceful degradation if unavailable. |
| Chabad.org relationship | Affects positioning and Phase 2 audio | Initiate complementary-positioning conversation early. No Phase 1 technical dependency. |

### 10.2 Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| AI generates halachically inaccurate content | Medium | Critical | Human review on all content. 7-day buffer. Chatbot grounded in approved sources only with kill switch. |
| Volunteer editorial pipeline cannot sustain daily cadence | Medium | High | Recruit 6–10 volunteers. Defined rotation. Degradation policy. 7-day buffer absorbs short-term gaps. |
| Content does not resonate with target audience | Low-Medium | High | Phase 0 validates demand before any application is built. Kill criteria defined. |
| Chabad.org perceives platform as competitive | Medium | Medium | Early conversation. Complementary positioning (visual/interactive vs. text/audio). No audio in Phase 1. |
| Scope expansion delays delivery | Medium | Medium | Phase 1 scope is locked. Features deferred to later phases are documented but not scheduled. |
